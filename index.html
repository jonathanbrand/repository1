Drone Witness Project.

To Observe and Discourage War Crimes

Call in the Drones

The Drone Witness Project allows people in a combat area to call in observer drones that witness and stream evidence of war crimes live online. When a person on the ground believes that war crimes are occurring, he/she uses a cell phone app to report it to an online dispatch coordinator. When available, a fleet of 6-12 aerial drones is dispatched from a location up to fifty miles away from the active combat zone. The dispatched UAVs work in tandem, gathering close and wide shots of the GPS coordinates collected and sent by the ground observerâ€™s cell phone app.

Live Streaming

Using a satellite data link, live video from each UAV is streamed to a website and recorded on a server so that it can be played back at any time by visitors. Video records are stored in incident folders organized chronologically.

The Purpose of Using UAVs as War Crime Recorders

The goal in capturing and broadcasting war crimes as they occur is to discourage military leaders from committing war crimes. A fleet of a dozen clearly marked, low-cost drones that broadcast video live to the web, should make it difficult to avoid public scrutiny in active combat areas.
